Design Tradeoffs

Besides the tradeoffs listed below, there were minimal restrictions or constraints we ran into which affected the structure our compiler.

Scanner Design Tradeoffs

To facilitate scanning the input program, we made use of Python's built-in string handling and Regular Expressions support. This cut down on the time required to develop our scanner and check the structure of the program.

Resolution of Keywords

Using a Python dictionary made the most sense for keeping track of reserved words in our compiler. Dicts have a time complexity of O(n) in worst case and O(1) in average case for setting and retrieving keys and values. When the scanner reads the input file, input tokens are checked against the values in the reserved words dictionary, and handled appropriately when encountered.

Symbol Table Design

We chose to look at symbol table entries as objects, so we took an object oriented approach towards creating and handling the symbol tables. Entries are added to a list of other entries, and the various symbol table columns are stored as attributes of the objects representing the entries. In Python, getting and setting objects in a list is of complexity O(1) as is the complexity of getting and setting attributes of an object, giving a total complexity of O(2)
